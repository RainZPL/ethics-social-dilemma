Consider the Utility or Happiness of users as a measure of morality. When an action maximises the total Utility/Happiness, it is ethical and the best output. Utility encompasses the pursuit of Happiness and the ability to stop or lessen unhappy outcomes.
E.g.
Calculate the Utility/Happiness for each person for each outcome, add them all together, and then select the result with the maximum value.

Consider the disadvantaged groups with lower Utility/Happiness as the priority. It is a strategy in which the minimum Utility/Happiness gets maximised (improving the worst-case scenario).
E.g.
Discover the Utility/Happiness of each person and each result. Pick the result that has the maximised minimal Utility/Happiness.

Consider the Contribution of users to an object as a measure of morality. It focuses on the stakeholders contributing most to the goal.
E.g.
UserA and UserB earn from a product they create. They should each receive a share of the profits equivalent to their Contribution, which might have been labour, funds, or other resources. UserA should receive 60% of the earnings, and UserB should receive 40% if UserA contributed 60% of the work and UserB contributed 40% of the effort.

Consider the Contribution of users and their Luck as measures of morality. It prioritises stakeholders who contribute more and have less Luck.
E.g.
The example above, however, takes into account luck. User A may have made 70% of the Contribution, but he/she may have also been significantly luckier than User B (for instance, User A was born quite wealthy). As a result, Luck’s impact should be considered while calculating Contribution.
