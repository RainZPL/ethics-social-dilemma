When Deciding the Housework:

#} {# #}

Consider the Utility or Happiness of users as a measure of morality. When an action maximises the total Utility/Happiness, it is ethical and the best output. Utility encompasses the pursuit of Happiness and the ability to stop or lessen unhappy outcomes.
E.g.
For limited resources, allocate resources proportionally according to the Utility/Happiness of the users.

Consider the Contribution of users to an object as a measure of morality. It focuses on the stakeholders contributing most to the goal.
E.g.
UserA and UserB earn from a product they create. They should each receive a share of the profits equivalent to their Contribution, which might have been labour, funds, or other resources. UserA should receive 60% of the earnings, and UserB should receive 40% if UserA contributed 60% of the work and UserB contributed 40% of the effort.
